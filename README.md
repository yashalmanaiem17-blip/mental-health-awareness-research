## What this module is

This is my individual Week 2 contribution to Group 43's SafeX Solutions research project. The module researches student mental health awareness gaps through a structured pilot survey and proposes an evidence-based awareness initiative, a Mental Health Resource Hub, for integration into the SafeX platform.

The module produces:

- A live, anonymous Google Form survey targeting students at high school, undergraduate, and postgraduate levels
- A cleaned, anonymised dataset of pilot responses
- A full Excel analysis workbook with descriptive statistics and charts
- A written report documenting methodology, findings, and the proposed initiative
- A short explanation video walking through the module

## How it works

The module has three components that connect end-to-end.

**1. Data collection using Google Forms.** An anonymous survey collects responses from students. The form has three sections: demographics, awareness and attitudes (including four Likert-scale statements), and a barriers and preferences section with two open-text questions. All questions are optional and no personal identifiers are collected.

**2. Analysis using Excel.** Responses are exported from Google Forms into Excel and pasted into the `Raw_Data` tab of `MHA_Analysis_Template.xlsx` with Likert text answers converted to numeric 1 to 5 values. From that single paste, the workbook automatically computes means, medians, agreement percentages, frequency distributions, demographic breakdowns, and barrier rankings across five interconnected tabs.

**3. Report and proposal.** The findings inform a written report that proposes a Mental Health Resource Hub for SafeX, built around three principles drawn from the data: anonymity (as the primary entry point), visibility (of institution-specific resources), and stigma reduction through peer normalisation.

## How to view or run this module

**To view the live survey:** open `GoogleForm_Link.txt` and click the link.

**To view the analysis:** open `MHA_Analysis_Template.xlsx` in Excel. Start on the Instructions tab. Headline numbers are on the Summary tab.

**To read the report:** open `MHA_Report.pdf`.

**To watch the explanation video:** open `Video_Link.txt` for the video link.
